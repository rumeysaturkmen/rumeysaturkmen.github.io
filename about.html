About

A short introduction.

PhD candidate at Concordia University, working on how people interact in 3D — without always needing their hands.

What I work on

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Concordia University, admitted directly into the doctoral program on the strength of my undergraduate research record. I work in the EXIT Lab under Dr. Anil Ufuk Batmaz, where my research focuses on controller-free interaction — eye-gaze and voice — for 3D selection and manipulation in virtual and augmented reality, especially in scenes that are dense or partially occluded.

My path here started during my undergraduate degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering at Kadir Has University in Istanbul, where I worked at HapLab researching eye-gaze behavior and haptic interaction. That led to a research internship at TUBITAK studying eye-gaze input for VR, and a term as a visiting research student at Dalhousie University's VERTEX Lab, working on 3D pointing and sketching in VR. Those experiences shaped the questions I'm still chasing today.
